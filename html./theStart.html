Character name: Your character's name will display when logging into a world, sending messages in chat, and whenever you die. It does not have to be the same as your Steam or console username.

Character difficulty: There are four difficulty levels: journey, classic, mediumcore, hardcore.

  • Journey: gives the player the ability to research and have resources like Creative mode. Journey characters can access the cheat menu by pressing C, they can enable Godmode, duplicate items, set the time and weather, disable enemy spawning, and more. Journey characters can only enter in Journey Mode worlds.
  • Classic: The player will drop some of their coins upon death. Depending on world difficulty, they may drop half, 75%, or all of their coins. They can be picked up by returning to the death location, represented by a red X on your map. However, players may have to defeat enemies to get them back.
  • Mediumcore: The player drops their items upon death. However, you can pick up your items at your death location, which is marked by a big red X on your map. If you die by explosives or lava, you may not find your items.
  • Hardcore: Means your player only has 1 life; if you die, the character file is permanently deleted.

  

World Size: World sizes come in three options:

  • Small: relatively compact and easy to travel; it will take you less time to travel between biomes and descend into caves. This makes the game easier at first, but you may have a hard time later on.
  • Medium: 50% larger than small worlds; they take a bit longer to travel through but have more space to generate treasure and extra biomes.
  • Large: twice the size of small worlds. It takes a long time to travel between biomes, but the size allows for nearly all possible terrain, treasure, and resources to generate in large quantities, unless they are extremely rare. These worlds are useful if you are looking to build.

World Difficulty: similar to character difficulty.

  • Classic Mode: offer the standard Terraria experience. Read the section on Classic Mode characters for a better understanding of a classic world.
  • Expert Mode: worlds have enhanced enemies and bosses, hazards, and valuable Expert-only goods. Many of the Expert-only items can significantly help you, and can be worth the challenge.
  • Master Mode: worlds have even more dangerous enemies and bosses, but don't provide much loot in return (besides vanity itmes) and one more accessory slot. All Expert items appear in Master Mode.
  • Journey Mode: for Journey Mode characters only; the cheat menu can be used. Note that Journey mode has difficulty sliders ranging from 0.5x (the easiest) to 3.0x (equivalent to Master mode). Sliding to 2x will enable all Expert mode features.

World Evil: In Terraria, there are two types of evil biomes, which spread throughout your world:

  • Crimson: a red-colored biome with themes of flesh and gore. Overall, its items are slightly more powerful, but the enemies are more dangerous. This makes it appealing to experienced players.
  • Corruption: a purple-colored biome that generally offers both lower risk and reward, making it ideal for new players.
  
  

Weapons you get:

  • Copper Shortsword: melee weapon
  • Copper Pickaxe: used to dig dirt
  • Copper Axe: used to cut trees for wood
  

Spawning Point:

If you click M on your keyboard, it will show you the map of your world. When you first join, it will be mostly back. No worries! That just means you have not explored yet and have not "seen" anything yet!

Movement and Controls

  • Walking/Moving: use the A and D keys to move left and right. Use W and S keys to descend or ascend. Please note that the W key does not allow you to jump.
  • Attack: click the LEFT mouse button to attack. At the beginning of the game, you are very weak, it is not recommended to fight UNLESS you are defending yourself. Stay sheltered at night.
  • Jump / Double Jump: use the SPACE bar to jump or double jump. Double jump is only possible with certain items.
  • Open/Activate click the RIGHT mouse button to open chests, talk to characters, etc.
  • Open Inventory use ESC key. The top row are the items in your "hand", the other rows contain items for storage. You can rearrange items aruond in your inventory.
  • Drop Item use ESC key. The top row are the items in your "hand", the other rows contain items for storage.

The Inventory

The inventory has certain slots that act a specific way.

  • Ammo slots: slots for ammo, used for ranged weapons. More examples to come in other sections
  • Coin slots: slots to put coins, including gold, silver and bronze. 100 bronze is worth 1 silver, 100 silver is worth 1 gold.
  • Trash slot: items can be disposed of when placed in the trash slot. Can press CTRL when clickling an item to trash it as well.
